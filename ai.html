AI in Healthcare

AI-Powered Diagnostics

Diagnostic imaging has been revolutionized by convolutional neural networks (CNNs), with models like InceptionV3, CheXNet, and DenseNet-121 leading the charge. CheXNet, for example, is capable of detecting pneumonia from chest X-rays with performance surpassing radiologists in some tests.

Google’s deep learning models detect diabetic retinopathy from fundus images with over 90% sensitivity. DeepMind’s mammogram-reading AI significantly reduces false positives and negatives in breast cancer screening.

Clinical Machine Learning & Predictive Support

AI-powered CDSS like Watson for Oncology and DeepMind Streams use patient vitals, EHRs, and genetic data to forecast sepsis, organ failure, and post-surgical infections.

Advanced models like XGBoost and time-series transformers help estimate readmission risk, medication efficacy, and progression of chronic illnesses like CKD or heart failure.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

NLP extracts key insights from doctor’s notes and pathology reports. BioBERT, ClinicalBERT, and Med-BERT outperform general models on biomedical tasks.

Chatbots like Ada and Med-PaLM assist in symptom triage and demonstrate expert-level performance on medical exams and patient communication.

AI-Enhanced Robotic Assistance

Robotic systems like da Vinci now integrate AI for suture planning and tissue recognition. Reinforcement learning allows surgical tools to adapt in real-time during procedures.

AI Interpretability & Clinical Trust

Tools like LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) and SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) help explain AI decisions to clinicians by highlighting what influenced diagnoses. This builds trust and encourages safer deployment in clinical settings.
